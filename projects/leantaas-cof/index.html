Centralized Operations Framework

Single source of truth for metric definitions across all LeanTaaS surfaces — define a metric once, and it applies everywhere.

Role Product Designer, Platform Team
Duration 2024
Team Platform Team, Analytics, Stakeholders
Tools Figma, Confluence

Problem

Metric definitions at LeanTaaS were created separately on each surface. A utilization rate defined in one product had a slightly different formula in another. An occupancy metric meant something different depending on which team built the view. Over time, this inconsistency compounded — stakeholders couldn't trust numbers that looked similar but weren't comparable, and the engineering cost of maintaining divergent definitions grew with every new product line.

The root cause was structural: there was no shared place to define what a metric was. Every team started from scratch, and the proliferation of slightly different definitions was a predictable result.

What I Did

The Centralized Operations Framework gives metric definitions a single home. Define a metric once — its formula, its logic, its edge cases — and it applies consistently across every surface that uses it. When a definition needs to change, it changes in one place and propagates everywhere.

Building the framework required aligning across teams that had been working independently. I worked closely with analytics leads and platform engineers to audit existing definitions, surface the inconsistencies, and create a governance model for who owns each metric and how changes get made.

Impact

COF is one of the platform team's most successful operational improvements. It's been widely adopted across product orgs — not because it was mandated, but because teams immediately saw the value of not having to re-solve definition problems that had already been solved. Stakeholders now have consistent numbers they can trust, and the engineering overhead of maintaining divergent metric logic has dropped significantly.
