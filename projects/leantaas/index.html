Nova Design System

Transformed LeanTaaS's component library into a scalable, token-based design system with dark mode and responsive foundations — self-initiated at the mid-level, officially adopted as a company initiative, and the foundation for all subsequent AI tooling I built.

Role Senior Product Designer, Platform Team
Duration 2022 — Present
Team Frontend Engineers, Infrastructure Engineers, Product Orgs
Tools Figma, Design Tokens, Slack, Confluence

Problem

When I joined LeanTaaS, there was no shared design language across the company's 4–5 product orgs. Each team had built their own patterns, resulting in a fragmented suite of products with inconsistent experiences. This created real business pain: the sales team struggled to sell products that didn't look like they belonged together, and LeanTaaS's acquisition strategy demanded a scalable framework for onboarding new products quickly.

Origin

Nova wasn't assigned to me — I self-initiated it. I recognized the fragmentation problem early and began building the system from the ground up as a side project alongside my core product work. What started as a personal effort eventually became the org-wide source of truth for design.

My background in fashion design from UC Davis shaped how I approached this work. I've drawn the analogy that a garment collection is essentially a design system — a coherent set of principles, materials, and patterns that create a unified identity. This framing resonated with LeanTaaS's CTO and helped non-design stakeholders understand the value of systematic design thinking.

What I Did

  • Built Nova from scratch as LeanTaaS's first company-wide design system, establishing a shared visual language across all product orgs
  • Introduced design tokens to both designers and engineers — bridging a cultural and technical gap that design systems work often fails to cross
  • Earned early buy-in from frontend engineers across three main products, including infrastructure engineer Bruce and frontend lead Casey, who became key collaborators
  • Became the recognized point of contact for all Nova questions across Slack and Figma, publicly introduced by my manager at a company all-hands as "the driving force behind Nova"
  • Currently partnering with Casey to migrate the IPF product into the Nova framework — the first real-world implementation of Nova at scale

Impact

Nova has since been added to LeanTaaS's official company initiatives list, driven by three converging business needs: sales cohesion across the product suite, experience alignment across product orgs, and a scalable onboarding framework for acquired products. What began as a self-started side project now shapes how a 90+ member tech org builds product. I was promoted to Senior Designer the following year — and Nova became the foundation for the AI tooling I built next.
