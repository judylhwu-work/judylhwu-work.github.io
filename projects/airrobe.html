Re-commerce · UX Design

AirRobe

As the first UX design hire, I owned AirRobe's entire user experience for shop.airrobe.com — redesigning the product search experience, building design system foundations, and improving global navigation with a focus on usability and information architecture.

Role User Experience Designer (First UX hire)
Duration 2 months (2021–2022)
Team CEO, VP Engineering, Lead Engineer, Art Director, CX Manager
Tools Figma, Storybook, Jira
Deliverables High fidelity prototypes, Wireframes, Journey maps

Overview

AirRobe reduces fashion's environmental impact by promoting Circular Fashion — focusing on reuse, recycling, and extending product life to cut water use, carbon emissions, and waste. Circular fashion emphasizes designing for durability, using recycled materials, and promoting repair and recycling to keep resources in use as long as possible.

As the first UX design hire, I owned the entire user experience for shop.airrobe.com, navigated UX best practices across the company, and built the design library foundations based on Google's Material Design.

Problem

The product search experience was difficult to navigate — inconsistent filtering, unclear listing states, and a checkout flow that didn't account for the platform's three distinct transaction types: Buy, Rent, and Peek. The primary focus was improving usability and the global navigation's information hierarchy.

Product Search Experience

Filtering system revamp

Filtering was completely redesigned with a focus on information architecture, accessibility, and usability. Listings are broken down into unique tabs — All, Active listings, and AirRobe Wardrobe — and stamped with their respective transaction tags: Buy, Rent, and Peek. Each item is also tagged with its size and condition for quick scanning.

Buy listings

To increase transparency, transaction tags also appear on the product page itself. Buying is straightforward — just hit the "buy" button. For users not fully ready to commit, "add to favorites" lets them defer the decision without losing the item.

Rent listings

The rent experience is cleaner and mirrors the buy experience. Users hit "rent," choose a date range, confirm, and check out — minimal friction throughout.

Private listings (Peek)

Items tagged "Peek" are private listings — not officially for sale, but open to offers. A simple modal lets shoppers show interest and make buy or rent offers directly to the owner. If the owner is interested, they follow up directly.

Details beyond the product description

Each listing now surfaces product specs — estimated retail price, label size and fit notes, and material composition. A size chart tab provides an international size comparison table, which is critical as AirRobe is building toward a global market beyond the US and Australia. Seller profiles include a bio, stats, reviews, and a map for direct pickup — giving shoppers more control over their own carbon footprint.
