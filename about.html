Hi! I'm a human-centered UX designer who leads with empathy, and have worked at companies of all sizes (from large companies like Accenture & Google, to early stage startups like Prenda, to stealth seed startups like AirRobe & Window). I have experience building and scaling teams, as well as experience leading my own research efforts and projects. I love getting to know customers and figuring out how to make their lives better through accessible UX design.

Educational background

I have degrees in Design (BA) & Managerial Economics (BS) from The University of California - Davis, and a UX certification from Springboard.

During my studies at UC Davis, I was able to dabble in all parts of design — including fashion, fashiontech, graphics, sustainability, textiles and typography. I had the opportunity to be a TA for one of the advanced fashion design courses, holding my own office hours twice a week. I also had the opportunity to be a Fashion Tech Lab Assistant for an advanced studies course with one of my professors. Through this experience, I was able to explore how technology can be applied to fashion. (Think Arduino Lilypads, tons of conducive thread, light-sensored LEDs, and whatever else I could get my hands on.)

My background in Managerial Economics has set me up wonderfully as a human-centered designer with an analytical mind, who makes important decisions based on actual data. Being a double major taught me how to persevere through challenging obstacles, as well as how to manage my time well.

Through Springboard, I was able to master skills in sketching, wireframing, interaction design, prototyping, and usability testing as part of a framework focused on the discovery, design, and evaluation phases of user-centered design. This bootcamp consisted of over 350+ hours of hands-on course material, which I completed over the course of 9 months (while still working full time at Google Play Apps). BARK app, GramCity, and Window are all projects created during the Springboard UX Bootcamp!
